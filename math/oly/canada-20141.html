Lower Bound on a Finite Fractional Sum

Let \(a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n\) be positive real numbers whose product is \(1\). Show that the sum \[\sum_{i=1}^n \frac{a_i}{\prod_{j=1}^i (1+a_j)}\] is greater than or equal to \(\frac{2^n - 1}{2^n}\).

Solution

Define \[P_n \coloneqq \prod_{i=1} ^n(1+a_i) \quad\mathrm{and}\quad S_n \coloneqq \sum_{i=1}^n \left[a_i\prod_{j=i+1}^n(a_j + 1)\right].\]

We first show that \(S_n = P_n - 1\) by induction. The base case is \[\begin{align*} S_2 &= a_1(1 + a_2) + a_2 \\ &= a_1 + a_1a_2 + a_2 \\ &= (1 + a_1)(1 + a_2) - 1 \\ &= P_2 - 1. \end{align*}\] Now suppose that the claim is true for \(n=k\). \[\begin{align*} S_{k+1} &= (1 + a_{k+1})(P_k - 1) + a_{k+1} \\ &= (1 + a_{k+1})P_k - 1 - a_{k+1} + a_{k+1} \\ &= P_{k+1} - 1, \end{align*}\] completes the induction.

The expression in the problem statement can be rewritten as \(\frac{S_n}{P_n}\). Since \(a_1a_2\dots a_n = 1\), by the AM-GM inequality, \[ P_n = \prod_{i=1} ^n(1+a_i) \geq 2^n\sqrt{a_1a_2\dots a_n} = 2^n.\] Therefore, \[\begin{align*} &\quad\frac{1}{2^n} \geq \frac{1}{P_n} \\ \iff&\quad 1 - \frac{1}{P_n} \geq 1 - \frac{1}{2^n} \\ \iff&\quad \frac{P_n - 1}{P_n} \geq \frac{2^n - 1}{2^n} \\ \iff&\quad \frac{S_n}{P_n} \geq \frac{2^n - 1}{2^n}. \end{align*}\]

\(\blacksquare\)
