Graph Connectedness Guaranteed

If a graph has \(n\) vertices, what is the minimum number of edges it must have to guarantee that it is connected?

Solution

The answer is \(\tbinom{n-1}{2} + 1 = \frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} + 1\) edges. We first show that our answer indeed connects any given graph.

Consider a graph \(G\) that has at least \(\frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} + 1\) edges and assume FTSOC that there exists some \(k\)-vertex subgraph of \(G\) (\(k < n\)), say \(G_k\), that is disconnected. The degree of each vertex in \(G_k\) must be at most \(k-1\). Similarly, among the remaining \(n-k\) vertices, since none of them connect to vertices in \(G_k\), each vertex will have a degree at most \(n-k-1\). Now if the number of edges in \(G\) is maximized, by the handshake lemma, the total number of edges in \(G\) must be at most \[ \frac{k(k-1)}{2} + \frac{(n-k) (n-k-1)} {2}.\]

Hence, if \(E\) is the total number of edges in \(G\), we must have \[\begin{align*} &\quad \frac{k(k-1)}{2} + \frac{(n-k)(n-k-1)}{2} \geq E \geq \frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} + 1 \\ \implies&\quad\frac{k(k-1)}{2} + \frac{(n-k)(n-k-1)}{2} \geq \frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} + 1 \\ \iff&\quad\frac{n^2-(2k+1)n+2k^2}{2} \geq \frac{n^2-3n+4}{2} \\ \iff&\quad k^2 - (k-1)n - 2 \geq 0 \\ \iff&\quad k - 2 \geq (k-1)(n-k), \end{align*}\] which is a contradiction.

It now suffices to show that \(\frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} + 1\) is the minimum possible bound. If the bound is \(\frac{(n-1)(n-2)}{2} = \tbinom{n-1}{2}\), then there is a counterexample of \(G\) as the union of a \(K_{n-1}\) complete graph and one disconnected vertex. On the other hand, there does exist a graph with \(\tbinom{n-1}{2} + 1\) edges; \(G\) can be a \(K_{n-1}\) complete graph connected to a distinct vertex by one edge.

\(\blacksquare\)
