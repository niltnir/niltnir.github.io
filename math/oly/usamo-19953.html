Perpendicular Bisectors and the Lemoine Point

Given a nonisosceles, nonright triangle \(ABC\), let \(O\) denote the center of its circumscribed circle, and let \(A_1\), \(B_1\), and \(C_1\) be the midpoints of sides \(BC\), \(CA\), and \(AB\), respectively. Point \(A_2\) is located on the ray \(OA_1\) so that \(OAA_1\) is similar to \(OA_2A\). Points \(B_2\) and \(C_2\) on rays \(OB_1\) and \(OC_1\), respectively, are defined similarly. Prove that lines \(AA_2\), \(BB_2\), and \(CC_2\) are concurrent, i.e. these three lines intersect at a point.

Solution

The main claim is that \(AA_2\), \(BB_2\), and \(CC_2\) are the symmedians of \(\triangle{ABC}\).

WLOG, we show that \(AA_2\) is the \(A\)-symmedian of \(\triangle{ABC}\). By the symmedian construction, it suffices to prove that \(\angle OBA_2 = 90^\circ = \angle OCA_2\). The given similar triangles yield \[ OA_1 \cdot OA_2 = OA^2 = OB^2.\] Then, we have \(OB^2 = OA_1 \cdot OA_2 \iff \frac{OB}{OA_1} = \frac{OA_2} {OB}\) and \(\angle BOA_1 = \angle BOA_2\). Hence, triangles \(BOA_1\) and \(A_2OB\) are similar. Since \(OA_1\) is the perpendicular bisector of side \(\overline{BC}\), \(\angle OBA_2 = 90^\circ\). These arguments can be repeated for \(\angle OCA_2\), so we get \(\angle OBA_2 = 90^\circ = \angle OCA_2\) as desired.

\(BB_2\) and \(CC_2\) are then also symmedians of \(\triangle{ABC}\) and it is well known that the symmedians of a triangle concur at the Lemoine point.

\(\blacksquare\)
