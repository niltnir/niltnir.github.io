Counter on the Edges of a Cube

On a cube, seven verticies are labeled with \(0\)s and the remaining vertex is labeled with a \(1\). An operation consists of choosing an edge of the cube and incrementing the numbers at the endpoints by \(1\). Can all the numbers be (i) equal, or (ii) divisible by 3 after a finite number of these operations?

Solution

(i) is impossible for parity reasons. Updating an edge increases the total sum of the vertices by \(2\), the initial configuration starts with an odd sum, and the the sum of the vertices in the desired configuration is even (\(8k\) where \(k\) is the quantity on each vertex).

(ii) is more interesting. Suppose we two-color the vertices of the cube, that is, color the vertices with two colors, say black and white, in such a way that adjacent vertices have different colors. Let \(B\) be the sum of the black vertices and \(W\) be the sum of the white vertices.

WLOG, assume that the \(1\) in the initial configuration is on a black vertex. We start with \(B=1\) and \(W=0\). An update to an edge increases each of \(B\) and \(W\) by \(1,\) so their difference is invariant (ie. \(B - W = 1\)). The desired end state where each of the \(8\) numbers is divisible by \(3\) requires \(B - W\) to be divisible by \(3\) so the desired goal is impossible.

\(\blacksquare\)
