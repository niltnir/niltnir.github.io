Parallelogram in Cyclic Quadrilateral

Let \(ABCD\) be a cyclic quadrlateral whose opposite sides are not parallel, \(X\) the intersection of \(AB\) and \(CD\), and \(Y\) the intersection of \(AD\) and \(BC\). Let the angle bisector of \(\angle AXD\) intersect \(AD\), \(BC\), at \(E\), \(F\) respectively and let the angle bisector of \(\angle AYB\) intersect \(AB\), \(CD\) at \(G\), \(H\) respectively. Prove that \(EFGH\) is a parallelogram.

Solution

We show that \(EF\) bisects \(HG\) and vice versa. To begin, let \(Z = XF\cap YH\). We then compute the value of \(\angle XZY\): \[\begin{align*} \angle XZY &= \angle EZY \\ &= 180^\circ - (\angle EYZ + \angle ZEY) \\ &= 180^\circ - (\angle EYZ + \angle EXA + \angle XAE) \\ &= 180^\circ - (\angle DYH + \angle FXB + \angle DCB) \\ &= 180^\circ - \left[\frac{1}{2}\left(\widehat{CD} - \widehat{AB}\right) + \left(\widehat{BC} - \widehat{DA}\right) + \angle DCB\right] \\ &= 180^\circ - \left(\frac{1}{2}\widehat{BCD} - \frac{1}{2}\widehat{DAB} + \angle DCB \right) \\ &= 180^\circ - \left(\frac{1}{2}\angle BAD - \frac{1}{2}\angle DCB + \angle DCB \right) \\ &= 180^\circ - \frac{1}{2}\left(\angle BAD + \angle DCB \right) \\ &= 180^\circ - 90^\circ = 90^\circ. \end{align*}\]

Thus we deduce that \(XZ\) is perpendicular to \(YZ\). Now consider \(\triangle{HXG}\) and \(\triangle{EYF}\). Segments \(\overline{XZ}\) and \(\overline{YZ}\) are each both an angle bisector and an altitude of \(\triangle{HXG}\) and \(\triangle{EYF}\) respectively. It follows that the triangles are both isosceles, and in particular, \(XZ\) bisects \(\overline{HG}\) and \(YZ\) bisects \(\overline{EF}\). \(E\) and \(F\) are on \(XZ\), and \(G\) and \(H\) are on \(YZ\) respectively, so the result has been proven.

\(\blacksquare\)
