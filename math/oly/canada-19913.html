Midpoints Lie on a Circle

Let \(C\) be a circle and \(P\) a given point in the plane. Each line through \(P\) which intersects \(C\) determines a chord of \(C\). Show that the midpoints of these chords lie on a circle.

Solution

Let \(O\) be the center of circle \(C\). The diameter of \(C\) passing through \(P\) passes through the midpoint \(O\), so the locus contains \(O\). Now consider the midpoint \(Q\) (\(\neq O, P\)) of some chord \(q\) passing through \(P\). Since \(Q\) bisects chord \(q\), note that \(OQ\) is perpendicular to \(q\). In other words, \(\angle OQP = 90^\circ\). As such, \(Q\) lies on the circle whose diameter is \(OP\). This is true regardless of the choice of \(q,\) so any midpoint \(Q\) will lie on the circle with diameter \(OP\).

\(\blacksquare\)
