The Foot is the Circumcenter

Given an acute-angled triangle \(ABC\) such that \(AB \neq AC\), let \(H\) be the foot of the altitude from \(A\) to \(BC\). The points \(P\) and \(Q\) are defined in such a way that if the three points \(A\), \(B\), \(P\) and the three points \(A\), \(C\), \(Q\) are collinear in this order respectively, then the four points \(B\), \(C\), \(P\), \(Q\) are concyclic and \(HP = HQ\). Prove that \(H\) is the circumcenter of triangle \(APQ\).

Solution

Let \(O\) be the circumcenter of \((APQ)\) and \(H'\) be the foot of the altitude from \(A\) to side \(PQ\). We show that \(H = O\).

First, observe that \(\triangle{APQ}\) is similar to \(\triangle{ACB}\) since \(\measuredangle PAQ = - \measuredangle CAB\) and \(\measuredangle APQ = \measuredangle BPQ = \measuredangle BCQ = -\measuredangle ACB\). Consequently, \(\measuredangle PAH' = \measuredangle CAH\), so \(AH'\) is the isogonal of \(AH\) (and vice versa) with respect to \(\triangle{APQ}\).

Next, we show that \(O\) lies on \(AH\). Since the orthocenter of \(\triangle{APQ}\) lies on \(AH'\), and \(AH\) is the isogonal of \(AH'\), the result follows. In other words, \[ \measuredangle QAO = 90^\circ - \measuredangle APQ = \measuredangle PAH' = \measuredangle CAH.\]

Finally, it is well known that \(O\) lies on the perpendicular bisectors of \(\triangle{APQ}\). In particular, consider the perpendicular bisector of side \(PQ\), say \(\ell\). \(O\) must be the intersection of \(AH\) and \(\ell\). Since \(HP = HQ\), \(H\) is preceisely that intersection. Hence, \(O = AH\cap\ell = H\) as desired.

\(\blacksquare\)

Motivation

The notorious fact that the orthocenter and the circumcenter are isogonal conjugates appears. This problem is a prototypical example of its application.
